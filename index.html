This page compares an audio clip from Protest the Hero's "Bone Marrow" with a song I wrote, comically named "Procure the Dinero" (a.k.a. Get Money).

The goal is to more closely examine the frequencies involved and see if I can identify contributing factors to tone, harmony, etc.

Bone Marrow - Outro

The first two images below show a spectrogram overview of 100-1100Hz and 100-3000Hz. There are quite a bit of overtones present, as well as a distinct representation of the base frequencies of the perceived melody.

Note: Deleting either the overtones or the base frequencies has a significant impact on the quality of the guitar tone, which suggests that the overtones are not solely the result of any added effects such as reverberation or detuning.

In the next two images, sample notes from the measure are examined. They appear to be approximately A5 and E3, a perfect 5th apart.
Does the entire melody maintain this exact interval between the two instruments, or does it vary from note to note?
Also note that the base frequencies (around the 300-500Hz region) are far less crisp and distinct, which probably contributes to a sloppier sound.

Procure The Dinero

Just as with Bone Marrow, the first two images show a spectrogram overview of 100-1100Hz and 100-3000Hz. Compared to Bone Marrow, it looks as though there are significantly less amplitude in the overtones, and also somewhat less in the way of subharmonics.
To what extent do these contribute to the perceived guitar tone?

In the next two images, sample notes from the first half of the measure are examined, and while not super in-tune, they appear to be approximately G4 and B3, a 6th apart.
Also note that the base frequencies (around the 300-500Hz region) are far less crisp and distinct, which probably contributes to a sloppier sound.
